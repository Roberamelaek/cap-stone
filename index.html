    Police Brutality Awareness in Minnesota

    Police brutality is a severe issue in Minnesota, highlighting systemic inequalities and the urgent need for reform. This website aims to raise awareness, share stories, and provide resources to combat police violence in the state.

    Statistics

    Deadly Force

    St. Paul Police Department has a deadly force incident rate of 9 per 100,000 people.

    Recial Disparities

    Black individuals are 5 times more likely to be killed by police in Minnesota compared to white individuals. Latino individuals face a similar disparity, being 4 times more likely to experience deadly force than white individuals.

    Police Accountability

    Only a fraction of cases involving police violence result in disciplinary action or criminal charges against officers.

    Story of Victims

    Police brutality has deeply affected communities in Minnesota, leading to numerous tragic incidents. These stories highlight the human impact of systemic issues in law enforcement, emphasizing the urgent need for reform. By sharing these narratives, we honor the victims and inspire action towards justice and accountability.

    Yia Xiong

    Yia Xiong, a 65-year-old Hmong man, was fatally shot by St. Paul police in February 2023. Xiong, who did not speak English and was hard of hearing, was holding a knife and did not respond to police commands due to the language barrier and hearing impairment. This tragic miscommunication highlights the need for better training and protocols for dealing with non-English speakers and individuals with disabilities.

    George Floyd

    The death of George Floyd in Minneapolis in 2020 sparked global protests against police brutality and systemic racism. Floyd was killed by a police officer who knelt on his neck for over nine minutes, despite Floyd's repeated pleas that he could not breathe. This incident underscored the urgent need for widespread police reform and accountability.

    Fong Lee

    Fong Lee, a 19-year-old Hmong man, was fatally shot by Minneapolis police in 2006. The officer involved was not charged, despite evidence suggesting excessive use of force. This case remains a significant example of the long-standing issues of police violence against marginalized communities

    Ongoing Resistance Efforts

    Justice for Yia Xiong Campaign

    Hmong elders and community members have mobilized to demand accountability and reforms in policing practices. This campaign includes protests, advocacy for policy changes, and community education to prevent future tragedies.
